# B2B Cold Outreach Copywriting

Generate personalized B2B outreach emails that get opened, read, and replied to.

## Email Frameworks

### AIDA (Attention - Interest - Desire - Action)
Best for: Introduction emails where the prospect doesn't know you.

```
Subject: [Attention hook — personalized to prospect's situation]

Hi [First Name],

[ATTENTION: 1 sentence referencing something specific about their company/role]

[INTEREST: 1-2 sentences about a problem they likely face]

[DESIRE: 1-2 sentences about the outcome/result, not the product]

[ACTION: 1 clear, low-friction CTA]

[Signature]
```

### PAS (Problem - Agitation - Solution)
Best for: Follow-ups or when the pain point is well-understood.

```
Subject: [Problem statement as question]

Hi [First Name],

[PROBLEM: Name their specific challenge]

[AGITATE: Describe the consequences of not solving it — what gets worse?]

[SOLUTION: How you help, with a proof point (number, customer name, case study)]

[CTA]
```

### BAB (Before - After - Bridge)
Best for: Prospects who need to envision the transformation.

```
Subject: [Before/After contrast]

Hi [First Name],

[BEFORE: Their current state — the pain they live with]

[AFTER: What their world looks like with the problem solved]

[BRIDGE: How you get them there — your product/service as the bridge]

[CTA]
```

## Subject Line Patterns (30-50% open rate targets)

| Pattern | Example | When to Use |
|---------|---------|-------------|
| Question | "Still doing [painful task] manually?" | Problem-aware prospects |
| Personalized | "[Company] + [Your Company] — quick thought" | Warm introductions |
| Stat/Number | "3x faster [outcome] for [industry]" | Data-driven prospects |
| Mutual connection | "Noticed you're also [shared trait]" | LinkedIn research |
| Curiosity gap | "The [industry] trend nobody's talking about" | Thought leadership |

**Rules**: No ALL CAPS, no excessive punctuation (!!!), no spam trigger words (free, guarantee, act now), keep under 50 characters.

## Multi-Step Sequence Template

**Email 1 (Day 1)**: AIDA introduction — personalized hook, establish relevance
**Email 2 (Day 3)**: PAS follow-up — deepen the pain, add social proof
**Email 3 (Day 7)**: Value add — share a relevant insight, article, or benchmark (no ask)
**Email 4 (Day 10)**: BAB — paint the before/after, direct CTA
**Email 5 (Day 14)**: Breakup — "Closing the loop" with soft final ask

## Personalization Hierarchy

**Level 1 (Minimum)**: First name + company name
**Level 2 (Good)**: + role-specific pain point
**Level 3 (Great)**: + company-specific observation (recent news, job posting, tech stack)
**Level 4 (Best)**: + mutual connection, shared experience, or content they published

Each higher level approximately doubles reply rates.

## Deliverability Best Practices
- Keep emails under 150 words (mobile-first)
- Plain text outperforms HTML for cold email
- Max 1 link per email (your calendar link or landing page)
- No images in cold email (triggers spam filters)
- No attachments (ever)
- Use your real name and title in signature
- Send between 8-10 AM recipient's timezone (Tue-Thu)
- Warm up new domains for 2-4 weeks before campaigns
